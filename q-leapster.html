Leapster

Information

History:

Released in 2003 by LeapFrog Enterprises, the Leapster was an educational handheld gaming system designed for young children (typically ages 4–8). It used interchangeable game cartridges and featured a stylus-based touchscreen, aiming to blend learning with interactive play.

Accomplishments:

  • Helped pioneer the educational gaming handheld market
  • Featured curriculum-based games focused on reading, math, spelling, and problem-solving
  • Durable, child-focused hardware design with parental appeal
  • Became one of the most successful dedicated educational handhelds of the mid-2000s

Specifications:

  • Developer: Leapfrog Enterprise

  • Manufacturer: Leapfrog Enterprise
